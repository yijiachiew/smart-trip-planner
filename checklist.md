
It’s organized into **Plan → Research → Prepare**, so you know exactly what to do.

---

# ✅ **UNIFIED CHECKLIST (With Common Tasks + Differences for Study / Work / Travel)**

---

# **1️⃣ PLANNING**

### **COMMON (for ALL: Study, Work, Travel)**

* Choose destination (country + city)
* Decide duration of stay
* Set total budget
* Check weather & seasons
* Decide travel dates (peak/off-peak)
* Confirm safety level of chosen area

### **STUDY — additional**

* Choose university/course
* Understand academic calendar
* Plan for orientation & semester dates
* Estimate study-related costs (tuition, materials)

### **WORK — additional**

* Confirm job type (full-time / internship / working holiday)
* Check salary vs cost of living
* Confirm job contract dates

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Decide travel style (shopping, adventure, nature, food)
* Identify key attractions
* Set daily spending limit

---

# **2️⃣ RESEARCH**

### **COMMON**

* Visa requirements
* Cost of living
* Transport system (metro, bus, taxi apps)
* Local customs & etiquette
* Currency exchange rate
* Mobile SIM / eSIM options
* Neighborhood safety
* **Detailed Attraction Research (Must-sees, Hidden Gems, Cultural Sites)**
* **Seasonal Context Analysis (Weather patterns, Festivals, Crowds)**

### **STUDY — additional**

* Campus location + nearby accommodation
* Course structure & workload
* Student insurance requirements
* Student discounts (transport, food, museums)

### **WORK — additional**

* Local labor laws & work rights
* Workplace culture & dress code
* Tax obligations
* Commuting route to workplace

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Best tourist areas to stay
* Attraction opening hours
* Local food must-try list
* Transport passes for tourists

---

# **3️⃣ BOOKING**

### **COMMON**

* Book flights
* Reserve initial accommodation (first few days/week)
* Buy travel insurance
* Arrange airport transport
* Prepare digital and printed copies of bookings

### **STUDY — additional**

* Secure student accommodation (if possible)
* Pay tuition deposit
* Book visa appointment
* Book required medical check-up

### **WORK — additional**

* Confirm onboarding date with employer
* Submit requested pre-employment documents
* Book work visa appointment

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Book hotels for full trip duration
* Reserve attractions/tours
* Book dining reservations (if needed)

---

# **4️⃣ DOCUMENT PREPARATION**

### **COMMON**

* Passport (valid 6–12 months)
* Passport photos
* Visa documents
* Flight confirmations
* Accommodation confirmations
* Insurance documents
* Emergency contacts
* Copies saved online & offline

### **STUDY — additional**

* Acceptance/offer letter
* Transcripts & certificates
* Tuition payment receipts
* Student visa requirements
* Medical/vaccination records

### **WORK — additional**

* Job contract
* Reference letters
* Resume
* Police clearance certificate (if required)
* Work visa documents

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Trip itinerary
* Attraction tickets
* Local map screenshots

---

# **5️⃣ FINANCES**

### **COMMON**

* Exchange some local currency
* Backup debit/credit cards
* Notify bank of overseas use
* Create a spending budget
* Consider using Wise/Revolut for international money

### **STUDY — additional**

* Budget for textbooks
* Course fees & materials
* Rent + deposit for student housing

### **WORK — additional**

* Budget for work attire
* Monthly savings plan
* Understand salary structure (tax, deductions)
* Local bank account setup plan

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Budget for attractions and activities
* Prepare emergency money
* Shopping budget (if relevant)

---

# **6️⃣ PACKING**

### **COMMON**

* Passport
* Wallet/cards/cash
* Phone, chargers, power bank
* Universal adapter
* Toiletries
* Basic meds
* Clothes suitable for weather
* SIM/eSIM (if pre-purchased)
* **Season-Specific Wardrobe (Layering, Fabrics, Rain gear)**
* **Activity-Specific Gear (Hiking boots, Formal wear, Swimwear)**

### **STUDY — additional**

* Laptop for classes
* Stationery
* Study backpack
* Light household items (optional)

### **WORK — additional**

* Work clothing (formal/casual depending on job)
* Laptop (if required for remote work)
* Professional shoes & accessories

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Travel-size toiletries
* Camera (optional)
* Daypack for sightseeing

# **7️⃣ ARRIVAL (FIRST 24 HOURS)**

### **COMMON**

* Clear immigration
* Buy SIM card
* Buy transport card
* Check in to accommodation
* Withdraw/exchange some cash
* Update family
* Locate nearest convenience store / medical clinic

### **STUDY — additional**

* Report to university / orientation
* Collect student ID
* Explore campus
* Begin long-term housing search

### **WORK — additional**

* Report to HR or supervisor
* Collect work ID
* Attend onboarding
* Set up workplace tools

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Review itinerary
* Confirm tour times
* Explore neighborhood
* Rest and recover from flight

---

# **8️⃣ SETTLING & DAILY LIFE**

### **COMMON**

* Create daily/weekly budget
* Learn local transport routes
* Buy groceries
* Set basic safety habits
* Explore the area gradually

### **STUDY — additional**

* Set academic routine
* Track assignment deadlines
* Join clubs & social groups
* Learn study resources on campus

### **WORK — additional**

* Learn workplace expectations
* Understand communication culture
* Plan commute
* Make colleagues/friends

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Follow sightseeing plan
* Try local food
* Keep valuables secure
* Adjust itinerary based on energy/weather

---

# **9️⃣ RETURNING HOME**

### **COMMON**

* Pack luggage
* Check luggage weight
* Confirm flight details
* Buy souvenirs
* Airport transport planning

### **STUDY — additional**

* Return library books
* Finish final assignments
* Close bank account
* End tenancy and collect deposit
* Cancel SIM

### **WORK — additional**

* Complete final work duties
* Return company equipment
* Collect final payslip/tax documents
* End rental and collect deposit

### **TRAVEL — additional**

* Check leftover cash
* Claim tax refunds (if eligible)
* Final shopping
